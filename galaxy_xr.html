Immersive XR platform safety system — ensuring users can explore virtual environments without physical risk.

Project Overview

Galaxy XR is an immersive extended reality platform developed in collaboration with Google. The project focuses on delivering safe and seamless XR experiences across devices, allowing users to fully engage with virtual environments while maintaining awareness of their physical surroundings.

My Role — Boundary System

I worked on the Boundary — a critical component of the XR binary responsible for guaranteeing that the user remains within a safe physical area during immersive mode. This system prevents users from colliding with real-world objects, walls, or furniture while they are engaged in a virtual experience.

The Boundary system is essential for user safety in any immersive XR application. It continuously tracks the user's position relative to a pre-defined safe zone and provides real-time visual and haptic feedback when they approach the edges of that zone.

The implementation required working at a low level within the XR runtime, dealing with spatial tracking data, coordinate system transformations, and rendering the boundary visualization in real-time with minimal performance overhead — all critical for maintaining the immersion and responsiveness that XR demands.

Key Challenges

Working on the Boundary system involved solving several complex engineering challenges: processing spatial tracking data with minimal latency, rendering boundary visualizations that are visible yet non-intrusive, and ensuring the system operates within the strict performance budget that immersive XR requires — every extra millisecond of processing directly impacts the user's sense of presence.
